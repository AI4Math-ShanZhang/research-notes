RECAP (π0.6): RL for VLAs

Paper: π0.6 — Physical Intelligence, arXiv:2511.14759. Full walkthrough: VLA_WorldModel/recap_rl_explained.html.

📄 Paper Summary

RECAP is the reinforcement-learning recipe behind π0.6, aimed at improving a vision-language-action (VLA) policy on real robots without the instability of on-policy policy-gradient methods.

  • Fit a value function offline. From logged trajectories (including human corrections), RECAP trains a value function — "how good is this state?" — with a Bellman-style update, rather than rolling out the policy live.
  • Extract the policy by advantage conditioning. Instead of pushing gradients through returns (PPO-style), it turns the value estimate into an advantage and conditions the policy on it, so the policy learns to imitate the high-advantage actions already present in the data.
  • Training objective. The final loss combines the value fit with the advantage-conditioned policy term, keeping updates close to the data distribution.

Compared with PPO, the appeal is stability and data-efficiency on real-robot data: no fragile on-policy rollouts, and human corrections fold in naturally as high-value examples.

🖊 My Key Notes

  • Value function = "what is this moment worth?" It scores a state by the return you can still expect from it — the single most reusable idea in the paper.
  • Advantage = "was this action better than average here?" Positive advantage → do more of it. Conditioning on advantage is a soft, offline-safe way to prefer good actions without unstable gradients.
  • Why not just PPO? PPO needs fresh on-policy rollouts and is twitchy on real robots. RECAP stays offline/near-data, which is why corrections and logged data slot in cleanly.
  • Open question for me: how sensitive is the policy to value-function error? A bad value estimate could reward the wrong actions — worth checking against the experiments.
