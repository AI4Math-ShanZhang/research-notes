Chunk vs. Event — one task, cut two ways

Task: "Pick up the red cup and put it in the sink." · Robot runs at 10 commands/sec · 30 steps ≈ 3 seconds

The robot's whole task is really just a stream of tiny commands — one every 0.1 s. Each command says how far to nudge the hand (x/y/z) and how much to move the gripper. Below is the entire task as 30 of these steps, colored by what the robot is actually doing at that moment.

Reach Grasp (close fingers) Lift Move to sink Release

Each little box is one action step. The number is a rough "how big is the motion" for that step. Notice the task naturally has 5 phases of different lengths — reaching is long, grasping is short.

Way 1 — Fixed-length CHUNKS (cut by the clock)

A chunk = always the same number of steps, no matter what's happening. Here: cut every 6 steps (0.6 s). The model predicts one whole chunk at a time, then looks and predicts the next.

The problem — boundaries fall in the middle of a behavior. Chunk 2 is half "grasp" and half "lift." Chunk 4 is half "move" and half "release." The clock doesn't care what the robot is doing, so a single chunk often mixes two different behaviors — which makes it harder for the model to learn a clean "this is what grasping looks like."

Good side: simple, every chunk is the same size, easy to batch. Bad side: the cut is blind.

Way 2 — EVENTS (cut at the joints)

An event = one coherent behavior, however long it takes. The cut happens where the behavior actually changes. Lengths differ: grasp is 4 steps, move is 9 steps.

The win — every segment is one clean, nameable thing. Each event holds exactly one behavior you can put in words: "reach," "grasp," "lift," "move," "release." Nothing is mixed. The model learns a crisp mapping from a word → a coherent motion, and the segment length follows the task instead of a timer.

Good side: clean, meaningful units that match language and vision. Trade-off: segments are variable length, so the training machinery has to handle that (which is a lot of what WALL-WM's engineering is about).
