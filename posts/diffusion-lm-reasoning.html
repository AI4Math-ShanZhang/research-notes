On the Reasoning Abilities of Masked Diffusion Language Models

Paper: On the Reasoning Abilities of Masked Diffusion Language Models — Anej Svete, William Merrill & Ashish Sabharwal (2025), arXiv:2510.13117. Cited as future work in the CoT-vs-Latent-Thought paper.

✅ In one line

A masked diffusion LM is a looped transformer with a token scratchpad, and its number of denoising steps is a depth dial: turn it up and you climb AC⁰ → regular languages → AC^d → NC. At any fixed step budget it is provably faster than one-token-at-a-time CoT on parallelizable problems — same computational power, fewer steps.

📄 Paper Summary

What a masked diffusion model (MDM) does

Start with a sequence that is all [MASK]. Then repeat for T denoising steps: run the transformer over the whole partly-filled sequence → predict the masked positions → unmask a batch of them (by confidence / schedule / randomness) → feed the sequence back in. Same weights applied over and over to a refining sequence — i.e. a loop.

The four models it relates

ModelDefinition
MDMMasked diffusion model — the process above; unmasks many tokens per step, in any order.
PLTPadded Looped Transformer — repeatedly applies a fixed block of layers (weight reuse → depth without extra parameters), plus padding positions that act as writable scratch memory.
CoTChain-of-thought transformer — writes scratchpad tokens one per step (any order, but one at a time).
pCoTParallel CoT — a CoT that emits P′ tokens in parallel each step; the interpolation between one-per-step CoT and full diffusion. (Not speculative decoding, which is about speed only.)

The core result

MDM ≡ PLT (finite precision, log width). A diffusion LM and a padded looped transformer are the same object: each denoising step = one loop iteration, and the still-masked positions = the padding/scratchpad. This is the bridge that lets every looped-transformer result transfer to diffusion, and vice-versa.

Confused how a token-writing MDM can equal a latent-passing PLT? The catch is finite precision: a “latent vector” only ever holds ~log N bits, which is just a few tokens’ worth — so committing to tokens loses nothing. And CoT’s weakness was never the argmax; it was doing it one position at a time. Walk through it with sliders: Bits, Precision & Tokens — a hands-on demo →

📐 Notation & preliminaries

Everything is stated in terms of circuit complexity classes. The one idea running through all of them: number of denoising steps = loop iterations = circuit depth = computational power.

SymbolMeaning
NSequence length (the input size).
fan-inHow many inputs one gate reads. Unbounded fan-in = one gate combines all inputs at once — massively parallel.
AC⁰AND/OR/NOT circuits, unbounded fan-in, poly size, constant depth. Weakest rung. Can do OR/AND of all bits and integer addition; cannot do parity or multiplication.
AC^dSame, but depth ≈ (log N)^d. The exponent d is the depth dial: more layers of unbounded AND/OR = more power.
ACUnion of all AC^d — the whole ladder.
NC^dLike AC^d but with bounded fan-in (2-input gates), depth (log N)^d. Interleaves with AC: NC^d ⊆ AC^d ⊆ NC^(d+1).
NCAll problems efficiently solvable in parallel (polylog depth, poly work). Examples: sorting, matrix multiply, graph connectivity, formula evaluation. NC = AC overall (same ceiling). This is the top of the ladder diffusion reaches.
Regular languagesWhat a fixed finite-state machine recognizes (pattern matching, parity, bounded bracket-matching). Sits at the NC¹ level.

The ladder (weak → strong): AC⁰  ⊂  regular languages (≈NC¹)  ⊂  AC^d  ⊂  NC.

Why circuits are the right lens: a transformer's attention is an unbounded-fan-in operation — one layer looks at all positions at once, exactly like one big AND/OR gate. So one forward pass ≈ constant depth ≈ AC⁰, and looping/denoising T times stacks T such rounds → climbs the AC ladder.

The five takeaways

Takeaway 3 (anchor) — MDMs are equivalent to PLTs. [Thm 3.1]

A diffusion model is a padded looped transformer: denoising step = loop iteration, masked positions = padding. Why it matters: the bridge — everything known about looped transformers transfers to diffusion and back.

Takeaway 1 — MDMs can do CoT (with overhead); CoT can simulate a diffusion run one symbol at a time. [Thm 3.3–3.4]

Two directions: (a) diffusion reproduces any CoT computation — it keeps the scratchpad power; (b) CoT can imitate a diffusion run, but only by unmasking one position per step, in whatever order diffusion chose. Meaning: same set of computable things — the difference is speed, not power.
Example: to spell MATH, diffusion unmasks {1,3} then {2,4} = 2 steps; CoT does 1→3→2→4 = 4 steps. Same answer "MATH," CoT slower. The gap is "how many per step," not which order — CoT already has the ordering freedom; it just can't parallelize.

Takeaway 2 — MDMs are provably more efficient than CoT on parallelizable problems. [Cor 3.7]

The strict separation CoT[log N] ⊊ MDM[log N, N]: with the same log-N step budget, there are problems diffusion solves that sequential CoT cannot. Meaning: the "depth beats size" gap, now proven for diffusion vs CoT — many-tokens-per-step is fundamentally faster than one-token-per-step, not just a constant-factor speedup.

Takeaway 4 — MDMs with log N denoising steps recognize regular languages. [Thm 3.2]

A concrete rung of the ladder. Regular (finite-state) languages need N sequential steps for CoT (walk the string left to right) but only log N steps for diffusion (resolve the automaton divide-and-conquer style). Meaning: a tangible log N vs N example.

Takeaway 5 — MDMs with O(log^d N) steps and poly(N) output = AC^d. [Cor 3.3–3.4]

The exact characterization behind Figure 2. "(log N)^d steps" = the step budget (depth dial). "poly(N) output" = polynomially many scratch positions. "= AC^d" = the power is exactly depth-d parallel circuits — no more, no less. As d → ∞ this sweeps out all of NC. Meaning: the paper fully pins down the expressivity, giving both the ladder and its ceiling.

Where diffusion sits vs loop & CoT

Writes per stepParallel?Stochastic?
CoT1 token❌ sequential✅ (samples tokens)
Looped TFwhole hidden state✅ parallel❌ deterministic
Diffusion LMmany tokens✅ parallel✅ (samples unmaskings)

Diffusion is the hybrid: parallel like the loop and stochastic like CoT — potentially strong at both of the tasks the CoT-vs-Latent-Thought paper separated (parallel computation and approximate counting). That is exactly why that paper flagged diffusion as the natural next thing to analyze.

Which real tasks are parallel vs sequential? (practical guide)

Deciding question: does the next step need the previous step's result? Yes → sequential (a forced chain, depth grows with size) → CoT. No, parts are independent → parallelizable (shallow, bushy, polylog depth) → diffusion / looped.

TaskShapeVerdict
Multi-step math word problem / proof (100→80→95→190…)each step needs the lastSequential → CoT
Evaluate one arithmetic expression (12+8)×(7−3)+5independent branches, shallow treeParallel
Image perception (objects, edges, regions)spatial grid, local & independentParallel
Image description / captioningindependent facts + thin fluency layerMostly parallel
Path-tracing / maze / spatial planningeach hop needs the lastSequential → CoT
Worked example — image description is mostly parallel. "A brown dog runs across a grassy field under a blue sky." The three facts are independent observations of different regions — "blue sky" does not need "brown dog" to be computed first, so they could be filled in at once (parallel). Two reasons it leans parallel: (1) the whole image is available at once — information isn't revealed step-by-step; (2) the facts don't depend on each other. The only sequential part is grammar/fluency (word order, agreement) — a thin surface layer, not a logical dependency chain like a math proof. This is why non-autoregressive / diffusion text generation works reasonably for captioning and translation but struggles on step-by-step math reasoning.

Contrast: text (a left-to-right chain) is sequential; an image (a grid of independent facts) is parallel — the deepest reason diffusion conquered images first but is still catching up on text.
Honest caveat. The parallelism isn't free — unmasking many tokens at once assumes they are roughly conditionally independent given what's revealed. When tokens are strongly coupled, aggressive parallel unmasking can produce inconsistent text, so real diffusion LMs still need enough denoising steps (enough depth) for tightly-coupled problems.

Fine print: positional encodings (where the power really comes from)

A positional encoding (PE) is the signal that tells each token where it is — attention alone is order-blind. The paper treats PEs as an external, precomputed input, so they can carry information the transformer itself is too weak to compute. This matters because the total capability is deliberately split between the PEs (indexing) and the transformer (the AC⁰ work on top).

Why the transformer can't compute modulo / division / binary

A fixed-precision transformer is a subset of AC⁰: constant depth, unbounded-fan-in aggregation. Such a model cannot compute parity (XOR of all bits) — the classic AC⁰ impossibility. Intuition: an unbounded AND/OR can ask "any 1?" or "all 1?", but never "exactly how many 1s?" — it's blind to exact counts. And modulo is exact counting mod k, division needs carry propagation (a long dependency chain), and binary representation is just repeated mod-2/divide-by-2. All need log-depth (a counting tree / carry ripple); constant depth is too shallow. Fixed precision makes it worse — it can't even represent a large index.

How the PE plugs into the loop / dLLM

The PE arrives with the hard values precomputed per position: binary(i), i mod k, i / k. Every loop/denoising step reuses the same weights, so what changes is addressing — which slot to read/write, with wrap-around like (i−2) mod N. That indexing is exactly the modulo/division the model can't do. So it looks up the PE and performs only easy AC⁰ ops on top (add the step counter, compare, shift by a power of two) to land on the target position, then attends there.

Why it's the prerequisite for looping: without correct indexing, iteration 2 wouldn't know where iteration 1 left off — the loop couldn't build on itself. Honest addressing from the PE is what lets T iterations stack into effective depth (AC⁰ → AC^d). Hence "L-uniform PEs are a prerequisite for looping to increase power beyond fixed-depth models."
Why this isn't cheating. The PE encodes only position and length (universal addressing, same recipe for any input of that size), computable in log-space (L-uniform) — not anything problem-specific. Some prior work smuggles entire circuits into the PE; here the PE just does the boring index bookkeeping the model provably can't, while the loop still does the real reasoning.

Cheat-sheet analogy: the transformer is a worker who can add and compare but can't divide; the PE is a printed cheat-sheet with all the division/modulo answers already filled in. With it, the worker finishes the job using only simple arithmetic + lookups.
