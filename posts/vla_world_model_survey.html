The central debate these papers are arguing about

Almost every paper here is taking a position on one big question: should a robot policy be built around a generative world model (imagine the future, then act), or should it map observations straight to actions (a direct policy)? And if it uses a world model, how tightly is action coupled to it?

  • Direct policy (no world model): observation → action, learned by imitation/RL. Bets on scale, data, and reasoning — not on imagining futures. π0.6, Qwen-VLA, Qwen-RobotNav, PhysBrain.
  • World model as context (one-way, optional): a generator produces goal images/hints that a direct policy consumes. π0.7.
  • One-way world-action model (video→action): imagine the future video, read the action off it; action does not feed back into the video. Protects the video prior. WALL-WM.
  • Bidirectional / action-conditioned world model: video and action interleaved; past actions feed into the world model. LingBot-VA(2), DreamZero.
  • Omnimodal world model: one model does forward-dynamics + inverse-dynamics + policy + generation across all modalities. Cosmos 3.
The second axis — granularity: where do you cut the action stream? Semantic events (WALL-WM) vs fixed-length chunks (nearly everyone else) vs waypoints (navigation). This is the exact "carve at the joints" debate you studied.

The 12 papers, by camp

① World-Action Models (imagine the future + act)

X Square Robot · arXiv 2606.01955

WALL-WM — event-grounded world-action model

Granularity: semantic EVENTS (variable length)Coupling: one-way video→actionAction: continuous flow-matchingReason: Staircase latent CoT (Qwen)Deploy: distill + FP8 → 10Hz

Unique: "carve at the joints" — the atomic unit is an action-grounded semantic event, not a fixed clock chunk. Video tower (from Wan) coupled one-way into an action tower; cross-view sight-cone + tube masking; two inference modes (event / unified).

vs prior: argues event-centric pretraining preserves the video prior and generalizes better than chunk-centric VLAs; beats π0.5, DreamZero, LingBot-VA on real-robot generalization.

Ant / Robbyant · arXiv 2601.21998

LingBot-VA — causal autoregressive video-action world model

Granularity: fixed chunksCoupling: bidirectional (action-conditioned)Action: continuous flow-matchingTrain: teacher forcing + causal maskDeploy: KV cache + FDM-grounded async

Unique: interleaves video & action into one causal autoregressive sequence (MoT); video conditions on past actions, action via inverse dynamics on predicted video. Teacher forcing works because robots retrieve real observations at test time. FDM re-grounds async predictions on reality.

vs prior: the action-conditioned counterpoint to WALL-WM — feeds action INTO the world model (this is the Figure-2 design you flagged). A WALL-WM baseline.

Ant / Robbyant

LingBot-VA 2 — native-from-scratch video-action pretraining

Granularity: fixed chunksCoupling: bidirectional (action-conditioned)Action: continuous flow-matchingArch: sparse MoE video streamDeploy: distill 2-step + FP8 → up to 225Hz

Unique: pretrains the whole stack from scratch (vs v1's retrofit of a video generator): semantic visual-action tokenizer aligned to a vision foundation model, MoE capacity, hierarchical VLM planner (~2Hz) + "Foresight Reasoning" async. Multi-Chunk Prediction fights myopic supervision.

vs prior: direct successor to LingBot-VA v1; beats π0.5 and v1 (RoboTwin 93.6 avg). Still chunk-centric & action-conditioned — opposite of WALL-WM on both axes.

NVIDIA · arXiv 2602.15922

DreamZero — "World Action Models are Zero-shot Policies"

Granularity: fixed chunksCoupling: joint/bidirectional (pixel+action denoise)Action: continuous flow-matchingTrain: joint video+action flow matching, teacher forcingDeploy: 38× speedup → ~7Hz, GT KV replacement

Unique: a 14B I2V diffusion model that jointly denoises video and action in one DiT; keeps imagination at test time but optimizes it hard (async, CFG-parallel, velocity caching, NVFP4, Flash 1-step). Ground-truth observations replace predicted frames in the KV cache to stop drift.

vs prior: breadth-over-repetition data; beats GR00T N1.6 & π0.5 (2×+ on unseen). A WALL-WM baseline. Chunk-centric & action-coupled.

NVIDIA · arXiv 2606.02800

Cosmos 3 — omnimodal world model for Physical AI

Granularity: chunks (32-step policy)Coupling: bidirectional, all directions (FD+ID+policy)Action: continuous flow-matchingReason: AR VLM reasoner (Qwen3-VL)Deploy: 4-step diffusion, skip video decode

Unique: one Mixture-of-Transformers that is simultaneously a VLM, video/audio generator, forward+inverse dynamics simulator, and robot policy — action is a first-class modality. The most general "everything in one model" bet.

vs prior: unifies the fragmented VLA+WM+dynamics pipelines; benchmarks against & beats LingBot-World, WALL-OSS, π0.5, DreamZero on RoboLab/RoboArena. The superset design.

② Generative backbones that feed the stack

Ant / Robbyant · arXiv 2607.07675

LingBot-Video — MoE video foundation model for embodiment

Type: video world model (base = video-only)Coupling: A2V variant = one-way action→videoTrain: flow matching + GRPO RL, 6 rewardsDeploy: DMD2 few-step distill, MoE

Unique: first large open-source sparse-MoE video diffusion model built for embodiment, with a physics-aware 6-reward RL post-train. The generative/world-simulator layer the LingBot family builds on.

vs prior: tops open-source embodied video-gen (RBench 0.620, Physics-IQ 40.4), beating Cosmos 3 & Wan on embodied/physics scores. It's the backbone, not a policy.

Ant / Robbyant

LingBot-Vision — spatial-perception vision backbone

Type: SSL vision encoder (no action/WM)Method: masked boundary modelingTrain: DINO/iBOT self-distillation + boundary loss

Unique: a ~1B ViT pretrained with "masked boundary modeling" — the teacher's predicted boundaries decide which tokens the student reconstructs, making geometry the native signal. The perception substrate of the stack.

vs prior: beats 7B DINOv3 on dense depth (NYUv2 RMSE 0.296); powers LingBot-Depth 2.0. Not a policy — perception only.

③ Direct policies (no / decoupled world model)

Physical Intelligence · arXiv 2511.14759

π0.6 — a VLA that learns from experience (RECAP)

WM: NONE (direct policy)Granularity: fixed chunks (50Hz)Action: flow-matching expert + discrete FAST tokensTrain: offline RL via advantage conditioning

Unique: RECAP — improves itself from real deployment data (demos + autonomous rollouts + human corrections) using advantage conditioning (not policy gradients). Learns a value/critic to score outcomes. >2× throughput, ~½ failure on hard tasks.

vs prior: the "learn from experience via RL" bet — the opposite philosophy to world-model imagination. Bets on real interaction, not dreamed futures.

Physical Intelligence · arXiv 2604.15483

π0.7 — steerable generalist with rich context conditioning

WM: decoupled (subgoal-image generator, one-way)Granularity: fixed chunks + subtask eventsAction: continuous flow-matchingDeploy: real-time chunking + async subgoals

Unique: diversifies the prompt/context — expressive subtask language, episode metadata (speed/quality/mistake), and multi-view subgoal images (from a separate BAGEL generator). Steer at test time via CFG toward fast/high-quality behavior; learns cleanly from mixed-quality data.

vs prior: has a world model but decoupled (one-way context hints), so philosophically direct-policy. Matches RL-finetuned specialists out-of-the-box.

Qwen · arXiv 2605.30280

Qwen-VLA — unified manipulation + navigation + egocentric

WM: NONE (direct policy)Granularity: fixed chunks/waypointsAction: continuous flow-matching DiTInterface: embodiment via text prompt only

Unique: one shared DiT action expert for manipulation, navigation, and egocentric human motion — embodiment is specified purely by a text prompt (no per-robot heads). Staged "text-to-action" pretraining bootstraps the decoder from language alone.

vs prior: unifies tasks/embodiments a single generalist; beats π0.5 on RoboCasa. Continuous, direct policy; RL via PPO on flow-matching log-probs.

Qwen · Qwen-RobotNav

Qwen-RobotNav — configurable navigation VLA

Domain: navigation (waypoints)WM: NONEOutput: 8 waypoints (regression)Reason: Qwen3-VL, spatial reasoning in-LLM

Unique: reframes multi-task nav as "observation-context modeling": one model + one trajectory head, with an inference-time reconfigurable observation interface (token budget, temporal decay, per-camera weights) an upper planner can switch mid-episode.

vs prior: a configurable executor inside an agentic system; SOTA on VLN-CE RxR, EQA. Nav differs from manipulation — outputs paths, no world model.

PhysBrain Team · arXiv 2605.15298

PhysBrain 1.0 — physics-commonsense-first VLA

WM: NONE (direct policy)Action: continuous flow-matching DiTIdea: physics as QA, not action imitationArch: dual-pathway (frozen general + trainable embodied)

Unique: builds a physically-grounded VLM from human egocentric video (structured meta-info → physics QA) before robot adaptation; robot trajectories become a thin adaptation layer. "Understanding first, action next."

vs prior: beats π0.5 on SimplerEnv & real Franka; bets on physical commonsense from human video, not learned dynamics.

The joint-training camp — all "joint", but over different scope

LingBot-VA, DreamZero, and Cosmos 3 all train video and action together (unlike WALL-WM, which freezes the video and trains in stages). But "joint" spans very different scope — how many modalities, and whether reasoning lives in the same weights.

Modalities jointly trainedUnderstanding in same model?ArchitectureObjectiveRole
LingBot-VA video + action (2) ✗ (separate VLM planner) dual-stream causal AR flow matching policy
DreamZero pixels + action (2) ✗ (System 1, no reasoning) one unified diffusion DiT flow matching policy
Cosmos 3 lang + img + video + audio + action (5) ✅ (reasoner + generator share blocks) omnimodal MoT AR + diffusion (hybrid) backbone
The ladder of generality: LingBot-VA & DreamZero jointly train just video + action (focused policies) — reasoning is separate or absent. Cosmos 3 jointly trains five modalities + understanding in one omnimodal backbone with a hybrid AR+diffusion objective — a general foundation model, not a single policy.

Contrast — WALL-WM is the odd one out: it does not train jointly. It trains the video tower first, then freezes it and trains action on top (staged) to protect the video prior. Same MoT-style separate parameters as the others, but freezing instead of joint optimization.

Mapped to the questions you asked

Every concept you probed during our WALL-WM / LingBot-VA deep dives shows up as a design axis across these papers. Here's where each lands:

"Chunk vs. event — where do you cut?"
Events: only WALL-WM. Fixed chunks: everyone else (LingBot-VA/2, DreamZero, Cosmos 3, π0.6/0.7, Qwen-VLA, PhysBrain). Waypoints: Qwen-RobotNav. → WALL-WM is the lone dissenter on granularity; the field is overwhelmingly chunk-centric.
"Is action fed INTO the world model (action-conditioned) or one-way?"
One-way (video→action): WALL-WM. Action-conditioned / bidirectional: LingBot-VA, LingBot-VA2, DreamZero (joint denoise), Cosmos 3 (all directions). No world model: π0.6, Qwen-VLA, Qwen-RobotNav, PhysBrain. → This is exactly the Figure-2 contrast you spotted: WALL-WM protects the video prior by NOT feeding action in; the LingBot/NVIDIA camp deliberately does.
"Discrete vs. continuous action tokens?"
Continuous (flow matching) dominates — all 8 policies use it. Discrete tokens appear only as an auxiliary signal: π0.6 & π0.7 also predict FAST discrete tokens for "knowledge insulation," but deploy the continuous action. → The field has converged on continuous; discrete (RT-2/OpenVLA style) is now mostly a training-side helper.
"Teacher forcing & the deployment latency trick?"
Teacher forcing + causal AR: LingBot-VA/2, DreamZero. Async + real-obs re-grounding (FDM): LingBot-VA/2 (Foresight), DreamZero (GT KV replacement), π0.7 (async subgoals + real-time chunking). Distillation for real-time: WALL-WM (10Hz), LingBot-VA2 (225Hz), DreamZero (7Hz), Cosmos 3 (4-step). → Everyone who imagines at test time needs an async + re-grounding trick to stay closed-loop and fast.
"Language-guided reasoning — is it a loop?"
VLM planner loop (re-invoked per event/subtask): WALL-WM (Staircase), LingBot-VA2 (~2Hz planner), π0.7 (high-level policy + coaching), π0.6 (subtask tokens). In-LLM reasoning, no explicit loop: Qwen-VLA, Qwen-RobotNav, PhysBrain (physics QA), Cosmos 3 (AR reasoner). → Hierarchical slow-reason / fast-act is now standard.
"Multi-view / cross-view handling?"
Dedicated cross-view mechanism: WALL-WM (sight-cone + tube masking, Camera RoPE) — the most sophisticated. Multi-view subgoals: π0.7. Simple tiling/concatenation: Cosmos 3, DreamZero. View tags in token stream: Qwen-VLA. → WALL-WM's geometry-aware masking is unusually deep; most others just concatenate views.

Big-picture trends

  • Continuous flow matching has won the action-representation debate — all 8 policies use it; discrete tokens survive only as an auxiliary training signal.
  • Two philosophies are actively competing: "imagine to act" (world-action models: WALL-WM, LingBot-VA/2, DreamZero, Cosmos 3) vs "learn from experience / scale the policy" (π0.6, Qwen-VLA, PhysBrain). Both post strong numbers — it's genuinely unsettled.
  • Within world models, the coupling is contested: WALL-WM stands almost alone on one-way + events; the LingBot/NVIDIA camp goes bidirectional + chunks. Your instinct ("the future depends on action") aligns with the larger camp; WALL-WM is the principled dissenter.
  • Deployment is now a first-class research problem: async execution + real-observation re-grounding (FDM / GT-KV) + few-step distillation appear everywhere imagination is kept at test time.
  • The stack is modularizing: the LingBot family shows a full stack — Vision (perception) → Video (world model) → VA (policy). Cosmos 3 bets the opposite way: collapse everything into one omnimodal model.
  • Reasoning is going hierarchical: a slow VLM planner (events/subtasks) over a fast action generator is now the default across camps.
Where WALL-WM sits: it is the most opinionated design here — the only one betting on semantic events + one-way coupling + geometry-aware cross-view masking together. Whether that principled stance beats the simpler chunk-centric, action-conditioned mainstream (LingBot-VA/2, DreamZero) is exactly the open question the benchmarks are still fighting over.
